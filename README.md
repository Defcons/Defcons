# Defcon

Software engineer based in **Norway** 🇳🇴, working across two worlds: **industrial automation &
critical control systems** by profession, and **full-stack web applications, developer tooling,
and self-hosted infrastructure** by craft.

Professionally I have close to a decade of delivery in the **oil & gas and energy industries** —
from offshore platforms and subsea control to operator tools — covering the full lifecycle from
design and factory testing through on-site startup and production support. That work is
safety-critical and doesn't live on GitHub, but it shapes how I build everything else. I also have
years of **scripting and automation in AutoHotkey**.

I care deeply about **safety and security**: I favour secure-by-default designs, careful handling
of data and access, and software that's tested and dependable enough to run in production. For me
**AI isn't a buzzword — it's how I work every day**: a lot of my projects are an ongoing experiment
in **AI-assisted ("agentic") software engineering**, directing AI coding agents to design, build,
and ship real software end to end.

## 🔭 Featured work

- **[clouddrive](https://github.com/Defcons/clouddrive)** — self-hosted file storage with a **Go**
  backend and **React + TypeScript** frontend: authentication & MFA, share links, granular
  permissions, trash/restore, and audit logging, packaged with **Docker**.
- **[epoglogs](https://github.com/Defcons/epoglogs)** — a full-stack combat-log analytics platform:
  a from-scratch log parser, a server-side ranking/percentile engine, and a custom wire protocol.
  *Curated, runnable, tested engineering showcase (AGPL-3.0).*
- **[epoch-sim](https://github.com/Defcons/epoch-sim)** — a damage/stat **simulation engine** in
  **Go** with an embedded web UI: deterministic combat modelling, statistical analysis, and gear
  optimisation. *Engineering showcase of one of my most-developed projects.*

I spend most of my spare time on **self-hosting and home infrastructure** — running services on a
Proxmox/Docker homelab, automating deployments, and building small tools to tie it all together.
